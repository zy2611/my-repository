My Favorite

Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift's journey as a music artist began at a remarkably young age, showcasing her talent and passion for songwriting from the outset. Born on December 13, 1989, in West Reading, Pennsylvania, Swift moved to Hendersonville, Tennessee, at age 14 to pursue her dream of becoming a country music star. Her first single, "Tim McGraw," released in 2006, marked her entry into the music industry and quickly gained traction on the charts. By the time she released her self-titled debut album later that year, she had already made history as the youngest artist signed by Sony/ATV Music Publishing and was well on her way to becoming a household name in country music.

With the release of her second album, Fearless, in 2008, Swift solidified her status as a major artist. The album featured hits like "Love Story" and "You Belong with Me," both of which topped charts and earned numerous accolades, including four Grammy Awards. Over the years, Swift has received a staggering 12 Grammy Awards and has been recognized with various honors such as the American Music Awards' Artist of the Decade and Billboard's Woman of the Decade. Her ability to seamlessly transition from country to pop with albums like 1989 and Red has showcased her versatility as an artist and garnered her a diverse fanbase.

Taylor Swift's songwriting is often lauded for its storytelling quality, drawing from personal experiences that resonate with listeners around the world. She has written or co-written every song in her discography, allowing fans to connect with her on an emotional level. Notably, songs like "All Too Well" have been praised for their intricate lyrics and profound emotional depth. Swift is also known for her philanthropic efforts, including education initiatives and disaster relief contributions, further solidifying her impact beyond the entertainment industry.

With nearly every song from her extensive discography ingrained in my memory, I can sing along from start to finish. Her versatility across various genres—from country to pop to indie folk—demonstrates her artistic evolution and keeps her sound fresh and exciting. Taylor's commitment to authenticity and vulnerability in her songwriting is something I deeply admire; it inspires me to embrace my own experiences and share my story with the world.

Taylor Swift is my all-time and overall favorite music artistand there isn’t a day that goes by without her songs playing in my music player. She has been my favorite for as long as I can remember. My admiration for her began at the tender age of most likely when I was just four years old, when I first heard her song "Love Story" from her album Fearless. Ever since that day, it marked her name in the ears of my heart, and the beginning of a lifelong connection, through her enchanting melodies and relatable lyrics. Swift has not only inspired me with her music but also introduced me to the art of songwriting when I was around 17, igniting my passion for storytelling through lyrics. Every song tells a story that resonates well with my life events. Nothing could ever compare to her when it comes to my favorite artist; she truly captured my heart and made me a poet who listens and learns how to write. Almost all of her songs in every album are integrated into my brain; I could sing every song from first to last. Truly no one could beat her as my favorite and inspiring artist.

Fearless

Taylor Swift's album Fearless, released on November 11, 2008, captures the essence of young love through its rich storytelling and relatable lyrics. The album explores themes of romance and heartbreak from a teenage perspective, utilizing fairy tale imagery to evoke the excitement and trepidation of first loves. Notable tracks such as "Love Story" and "You Belong With Me" not only achieved commercial success but also resonated with listeners who have experienced the highs and lows of adolescent relationships. The album won multiple accolades, including Album of the Year at the Grammy Awards, solidifying Swift's place in music history.

For me, Fearless evokes memories of my own teenage experiences filled with unrequited love and the fear of vulnerability. The longing to send a love letter without fear of rejection resonates deeply; I often found myself wishing I could embody that same fearless spirit when it came to expressing my feelings. Songs like "Breathe" and "Forever and Always" mirror my struggles with affection and heartbreak, reminding me of the emotional rollercoaster that comes with young love. The album encapsulates those bittersweet moments of yearning and hope, allowing me to relive my teenage years as I navigate through my own relationships.

My favorite tracks from Fearless—"Fearless," "Love Story," "You Belong With Me," "Breathe," "You're Not Sorry," "The Way I Loved You," "Forever and Always," "Come In With the Rain," and "The Other Side of the Door —serve as a soundtrack to my youthful aspirations and heartaches. They remind me that while I may not have been fearless in expressing my feelings back then, the journey of self-discovery has taught me that vulnerability is a strength. Listening to this album allows me to embrace those memories with a sense of nostalgia and empowerment, as I continue to learn about love and friendship in my life today.

Speak Now

Released on October 25, 2010, Speak Now is Taylor Swift's third studio album and the first one she wrote entirely by herself. This self-penned project solidified her reputation as a gifted storyteller, with songs capturing love, heartbreak, and personal growth through vivid narratives. The album’s title reflects the recurring theme of moments when people must seize the opportunity to speak their hearts. With a mix of country-pop and orchestral elements, Speak Now was both a critical and commercial success, debuting at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and earning multiple Grammy nominations.

The album is filled with deeply personal songs, including the heart-wrenching apology in "Back to December" and the emotionally raw "Dear John," rumored to be about a famous ex. Speak Now resonates deeply with my social experiences, particularly the pain of feeling betrayed and the struggle to voice my side of the story. I have often found myself wishing I could speak up and share my feelings, especially during moments when I felt overlooked or unheard. This album reminds me that it's never too late to reclaim my narrative and embrace who I am, even if it means confronting difficult truths.

My favorite songs from Speak Now—"Back to December," "Dear John," "Enchanted," "Innocent," "Haunted," "Superman," and "Timeless" "Enchanted" stands out as a fairytale-inspired ballad that perfectly captures the magic of a fleeting connection, while tracks like "Haunted" explore darker, more dramatic tones. Swift also showcased her optimism in songs like "Superman" and "Timeless," which reflect her hopes for enduring love. The song "Innocent" particularly resonates with me as I embrace the idea that it's never too late for renewal and transformation—especially being born in September, a month that symbolizes new beginnings. As mention in the song that says "You'll have new Septembers", I do hope I'll have them sooner.

RED

Red, released on October 22, 2012, is an emotional masterpiece that captures the messy, passionate, and devastating rollercoaster of love and heartbreak. Often considered one of Taylor Swift’s most poignant and relatable albums, and also my overall favorite album, Red explores the highs and lows of relationships, from euphoric beginnings to heartbreaking ends. With its eclectic mix of pop, country, and rock influences, it perfectly encapsulates the chaos and beauty of love, leaving listeners with vivid imagery and raw emotions. The album is a testament to the unpredictability of love, where every song feels like a chapter in a deeply personal story. The album's title reflects the intensity of emotions Swift explores within—love, heartbreak, and everything in between—often comparing her experiences to the vivid colors of red, symbolizing passion, anger, and joy. With the fan favorite hit “All Too Well,” Red captivated listeners with its raw vulnerability and intricate songwriting.

Listening to Red resonates deeply with my own experiences of heartbreak and the struggle to let go. The album's themes reflect the pain of trying to revive something that has been irreparably damaged, mirroring my journey through relationships that have left me feeling broken. It’s a mirror of my own experiences with love and heartbreak. It beautifully tells the story of someone experiencing the greatest heartbreak of their life, whether through love, friendship, or any deeply cherished bond. Songs like “Sad Beautiful Tragic” and “I Almost Do” capture the excruciating pain of wanting to reach out to someone you once loved but holding back because the wounds are too raw, and the thought of reopening them feels unbearable. Meanwhile, “All Too Well,” especially its 10-minute version, delves into the bittersweet ache of revisiting a love that left a permanent scar—a story you can’t help but replay, remembering every detail and emotion as if it were yesterday. This album feels like a companion during those shattered moments, reminding me of the bravery required to finally let go of the past.

This album feels like the epitome of my relationships, as if my life revolves around the emotions it captures. Songs like “Forever Winter” remind me of the importance of being there for someone battling their struggles, while “Come Back… Be Here” echoes the longing for what could have been. My favorite songs are “State of Grace,” “Red,” “Treacherous,” “I Knew You Were Trouble,” “All Too Well”, “Holy Ground,” “Sad Beautiful Tragic,” “The Lucky One,” “The Moment I Knew,” “Come Back… Be Here,” “Better Man,” “Babe,” and “Forever Winter.” Truly, almost every song is my favorite, which is why Red stands as my almost overall favorite Taylor Swift album, a constant reminder of the beauty and pain intertwined in love. Each song feels like a chapter in my life story, making Red not just an album but a reflection of my own experiences with love and resilience. Almost every song in this album is my favorite, no wonder it’s my almost overall favorite! Live, laugh, and find love, Red!

1989

1989, released on October 27, 2014, is Taylor Swift’s full embrace of pop music, marking a bold departure from her country roots. Inspired by the '80s synth-pop, 1989 is a dazzling, infectious album filled with upbeat anthems and introspective ballads. It’s a narrative of reinvention, capturing Taylor’s transition from country singer-songwriter to a global pop icon, marks one of the most iconic transitions in Taylor Swift's career. With its sleek production, catchy hooks, and emotionally charged lyrics, this transformation was not only seamless but also celebrated, with 1989 becoming a cultural phenomenon, earning Taylor widespread acclaim and a Grammy Award for Album of the Year.

This album offers comfort and assurance, reminding me not to rush into love. It’s a testament to embracing singlehood as a time of freedom, fun, and self-discovery. Taylor captures this sentiment perfectly, especially in songs like “New Romantics,” which paints a vivid picture of living in the moment, surrounded by friends, and savoring life without the weight of heartbreak or romantic expectations. Taylor herself described this album as a reflection of her life in New York with her closest friends, and that spirit of independence and celebration radiates through every track. 1989 reminds me that being single isn’t about missing out—it’s about learning to find joy and fulfillment within yourself and with the people who truly matter.

Every song on this album feels like a polished gem, brimming with energy and introspection. My favorite tracks include “Blank Space,” “Style,” which is not only my favorite song on the album but possibly also my overall favorite from Taylor Swift's entire catalog. “Out of the Woods” and “Wildest Dreams” dive into romantic nostalgia and reminiscing a long gone love affair, while “I Wish You Would” and “All You Had to Do Was Stay” explore the complexities of love and regret. “New Romantics” serves as an upbeat anthem of embracing freedom, and the hauntingly beautiful “Is It Over Now?” delves into the aftermath of a broken relationship. “I Know Places,” and “Wonderland,” with its timeless themes and irresistible beats, it’s no wonder 1989 remains one of the most unforgettable chapters in Taylor Swift’s discography. 1989 is a complete package, where every song offers something special, and for me, it’s the perfect pop album.

reputation

Reputation, released on November 10, 2017, showcases a darker and edgier side of Taylor Swift, reflecting themes of reinvention, resilience, and self-empowerment. This album marked a pivotal moment in Taylor’s career, as she embraced a more electronic and urban sound, blended with her signature storytelling. Reputation delves into public perception, love, and the complexities of fame, with Taylor reclaiming her narrative amidst media scrutiny, particularly following her rise to fame after 1989. Thematically, Reputation explores concepts of anger, vengeance, and the complexities of love amidst chaos, creating a narrative that resonates deeply with listeners who have faced personal trials. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, solidifying her status as a pop powerhouse.

On a personal level, Reputation resonates profoundly with my own experiences of ridicule and betrayal. Like Swift, I have faced moments where those I trusted turned against me, leading to feelings of isolation and despair. The album's exploration of reclaiming one's narrative mirrors my journey of self-discovery after being hurt by someone I loved. It emphasizes the importance of self-worth and the realization that love should not be contingent on others' opinions or actions. Through the stormy battles of life, I learned to embrace my own value, much like Swift's transformation throughout this album.

My favorite tracks from Reputation—"...Ready for It?", "Delicate", "Look What You Made Me Do", "So It Goes...", "Getaway Car", and "Call It What You Want"—serve as anthems of resilience and empowerment. Each song encapsulates a facet of my journey: from the initial chaos to finding solace in self-love. Ultimately, Reputation is not just an album; it is a powerful reminder that our worth is inherent and that we can rise above the challenges life throws our way.

Lover

Lover marks a dreamy and vibrant chapter in Taylor Swift’s discography, offering a celebration of love, self-reflection, and resilience. While it may not be among my absolute top picks, this album stands out for its timeless classics and its ability to remind me that love doesn’t have to burn like red—it can be golden, as Taylor so beautifully conveys in "Daylight." Despite my own constant heartbreaks and disappointments from various relationships, Lover is a beacon of hope, proving that love, even in a world full of pain, still exists and has its moments of brilliance.

This album feels like a checkpoint in my life. Though I haven't yet fully resonated with all its messages, I sense that when I meet someone I can truly call my “lover,” Lover will reveal its full emotional depth to me. The track "Daylight" is my absolute favorite—it speaks of hope after dark times, reminding me that the golden light of the rising sun always follows the night. Its closing line, "I wanna be defined by the things that I love, not the things I hate," inspires me to focus on the good and let go of past hurt.

Other tracks that speak deeply to me include "The Archer," which captures the fear of vulnerability, the anxiety of being left behind, and the desperate hope that someone will stay through the battles. My favorites also include "Death By a Thousand Cuts," a poignant anthem of heartbreak; "False God," with its sultry undertones of faith in a fragile connection; "The Man," a powerful critique of gender dynamics; and "Cornelia Street," a nostalgic ode to places tied to unforgettable memories. Together, these songs create a kaleidoscope of emotions that makes Lover a unique and deeply personal experience.

folklore

folklore, released on July 24, 2020, stands as one of Taylor Swift’s most acclaimed and introspective works. Written and produced largely during the COVID-19 pandemic, the album marked a significant departure from Swift’s earlier pop sound, embracing a stripped-back, indie-folk aesthetic that showcased her storytelling prowess. Folklore holds a special place in my heart, ranking among my top three favorite Taylor Swift albums. It’s a masterpiece that awakened my love for poetry and storytelling, with its nostalgic, introspective, and slightly haunting narratives wrapped in a rustic, cottagecore aesthetic. This album resonates deeply with my inner child, offering solace and creative inspiration through its tales of love, loss, and self-reflection. The wistful and tender tone of folklore feels like opening an old diary, where every song is a page brimming with emotion and imagery.

One of my absolute favorite tracks is "cardigan," which paints the heartbreaking story of a young woman betrayed by someone who once made her feel like the universe was hers. I see so much of myself in this song—always wanting to believe I could be someone’s favorite, only to end up forgotten, like a cardigan left under someone's bed. It perfectly encapsulates the bittersweet reality that those who bring you the most joy can also bring you the deepest pain. And yet, despite the heartbreak, there’s a yearning for them to come back, even though you know it might not be the best choice. The second piece of the trilogy, "august," captures the feeling of being second best—the fleeting and fragile love of being someone’s option but not their priority. It’s the aching realization of accepting scraps of affection just to feel close to them, even if it’s not enough to truly fulfill you. The track "august" reflects that longing for something more while settling for less, the quiet desperation of holding onto moments you know are temporary.

Other tracks on folklore strike deeply personal chords. "My Tears Ricochet" feels like an anthem for anyone whose sense of home has crumbled, a poignant question of whether the person who hurt you feels the weight of their actions. The song "seven" intertwines with my inner child, mourning the lost innocence and joy I couldn’t fully embrace. The songs "this Is Me Trying" and "mirrorball" are my holy anthems—reflections of feeling average, like an underachiever, constantly striving for validation and yet feeling like you’ll never measure up. "Invisible String" rekindles my hopeless romantic heart, offering the comforting thought that perhaps, somewhere, my destined person exists in a place I’d never expect. Other favorites like "Illicit Affairs," "The Last Great American Dynasty," and "Mad Woman" continue to unravel stories of betrayal, resilience, and independence, each woven with raw emotion and poetic brilliance. Folklore isn’t just an album—it’s a sanctuary, a testament to the beauty of storytelling, and a reminder of how profoundly music can mirror our lives.

evermore

Taylor Swift's album evermore, released on December 11, 2020, serves as the sister album to her critically acclaimed folklore, expanding upon the introspective and narrative-driven style established in its predecessor. Musically, evermore blends genres such as indie folk, alternative rock, and chamber pop, creating a rich tapestry of sound that complements its lyrical themes. The album features an anthology of stories revolving around love, loss, infidelity, and the complexities of human emotion. Tracks like "willow" and "champagne problems" showcase Swift's signature storytelling prowess, drawing listeners into the emotional landscapes she crafts with her words.

Being folklore's sister album, evermore shares a resonating vibe that makes it equally cherished in my heart. The two albums fit together like puzzle pieces; without one, the other wouldn’t feel complete. One standout track for me is "ivy," which beautifully captures the turmoil of being involved in a secret affair. It resonates deeply with my experiences of losing affection for someone while developing feelings for another, evoking the suffocating nature of hidden love. Additionally, "marjorie," a heartfelt tribute to Swift's late grandmother, strikes a personal chord as I also lost my grandmother at a young age. The poignant lyrics evoke memories of longing and the fading of cherished moments, leaving me emotionally moved.

Other favorites from evermore include "gold rush," which explores the feeling of falling for someone seemingly out of reach, and "tolerate it," which reflects the pain of feeling merely tolerated rather than truly loved. Songs like "long story short" resonate with my journey of overcoming chaos, while "no body no crime" offers an intriguing narrative that feels cinematic in its storytelling. The nostalgic essence of "’tis the damn season" captures the bittersweet feeling of returning to familiar places and faces. Overall, evermore encapsulates a spectrum of emotions and experiences that mirror my own life, making it a deeply meaningful addition to my musical journey.

Midnights

Taylor Swift's album Midnights, released on October 21, 2022, stands out as a deeply introspective exploration of sleepless nights and the myriad emotions that accompany them. Described by Swift as a collection of songs inspired by her "13 sleepless nights," the album delves into themes of self-reflection, love, regret, and the haunting echoes of past relationships. Musically, Midnights blends synth-pop and indie sounds, creating an atmospheric backdrop that complements its lyrical depth. Tracks like "Lavender Haze" and "Anti-Hero" showcase Swift's ability to balance catchy melodies with poignant storytelling, reflecting her growth as both an artist and a person.

One of my favorite songs from Midnights is "Would've, Could've, Should've," which resonates with me on a profound level as it connects back to her earlier work in Speak Now. The haunting nature of this track captures the struggle of letting go of past relationships and the lingering pain caused by someone you once trusted. I can relate to this feeling all too well; it makes me reflect on how my life might have been different had I never crossed paths with certain individuals. Another standout for me is "You're Losing Me," which poignantly depicts the slow unraveling of love when only one person is invested in making it work. This song encapsulates the heart-wrenching realization that love requires mutual effort, and it beautifully narrates the pain of feeling unseen.

Additionally, "Anti-Hero" strikes a chord as it reveals Swift's vulnerability amidst her confident exterior, mirroring my own insecurities about not being good enough. The album also features empowering tracks like "You're On Your Own, Kid," which celebrates independence and the strength found in facing life's challenges alone. Songs like "Labyrinth" and "The Great War" highlight the beauty of falling in love again after enduring heartbreak, while "High Infidelity" explores the complexities of trust in relationships. Overall, Midnights captures a spectrum of emotions that resonate deeply with my own experiences, making it a significant addition to my musical journey.

The Tortured Poets Department

Taylor Swift's album The Tortured Poets Department, released on April 19, 2024, might just rival Red as my overall favorite. With a staggering 31 tracks, nearly every song resonates with me, making it feel like a treasure trove of emotional expression. The album's title reflects the essence of my own experiences with heartbreak and ridicule, capturing the feeling of being a "tortured poet." This collection serves as a cathartic outlet for those of us who have faced life's challenges, and the double album format feels like a generous gift to fans, allowing us to indulge in Swift's lyrical storytelling.

One standout track for me is "Who's Afraid of Little Old Me?" This song resonates deeply as it channels the feelings of someone who has been tormented in profound ways. It evokes the spirit of Carrie White from my favorite movie, encapsulating the struggle against suffocating negativity from others. Every time I listen to it, I feel a powerful release, as if I am screaming out my own experiences of torment and resilience. Similarly, "The Prophecy" captures the yearning for change amidst misfortunes—a sentiment I often grapple with as I navigate my own hopes and dreams. Another favorite is "So Long, London," which poignantly addresses the difficulty of bidding farewell to something cherished from my youth. The bittersweet nostalgia resonates with my own experiences of letting go while wishing for the best for what once was. Additionally, "I Can Do It With a Broken Heart" speaks to the strength required to push through pain while maintaining a façade of resilience. This song perfectly articulates the struggle of handling heartbreak in silence while wearing a brave face.

Tracks like "How Did It End?" and "Cassandra" further illustrate the complexities of relationships and how simple actions can lead to irreversible consequences. Other favorites such as "My Boy Only Breaks His Favorite Toys," "Florida!!!," "Guilty as Sin?," and "thanK you aIMee" showcase Swift's ability to blend humor with deep emotional truths. Ultimately, The Tortured Poets Department stands as a new masterpiece in Taylor Swift's discography, destined to be cherished by future poets who discover its profound insights into love and life’s trials.
